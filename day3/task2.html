soil:

Soil conservation is the practice of using methods to prevent soil degradation, protect it from erosion by wind and water, and maintain its fertility for sustainable use. Key techniques include cover cropping, which adds organic matter and prevents erosion, and no-till farming, which reduces disturbance to the soil structure. Other methods like terracing, crop rotation, and windbreaks further protect soil, enhance water quality, support biodiversity, and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.

Soil conservation is a set of farming methods and practices that keep the land from degradation, erosion, and depletion. Soil conservation management targets long-term use with an eye toward the future. By taking proper and timely actions, farmers boost the performance of their fields for years to come. A major objective of soil conservation is maintaining the biodiversity of the eco-communities that inhabit it and contribute to its fertility in their own ways.
